ALTAAF'S ABSURDIST ADVENTURES

You don't know what you've missed!

life excerpts

Well, first off, I’ve gotta go on a little tangent first – Windows 11, you’ll get your comeuppance someday, rahhhh! Picture this, I was setting up a bootable drive for Ubuntu last week – it was for school – and tested it on my laptop. Chose the pen-drive as the boot up and then put on a video on YouTube whilst waiting for it to set up. Five minutes later, I look at my screen, and it’s turned off. Powered up the laptop again, made sure to choose the correct boot up, and then just looked at the screen to make sure nothing goes wrong.

Error message: Violation of security policy (or something like that pops up).

The genius that I am then decides to turn off secure booting to try to fix this, and lo and behold, once I try to get back to Windows, I find that I have been locked out of my own laptop, with the decryption key nowhere to be found. I scoured through all my Microsoft accounts, with every possible email address that I can think of, in the hope of finding a key stashed somewhere; but with no luck, I had to resign myself to installing Windows all over again.

If you think that was the end of the story, you haven’t even scraped the surface of my usual clumsiness – installing the OS was easy, having no drivers installed on it, on the other hand, was Lady Luck’s way of telling me how much she loves me :*

In the end, I spent 16 hours trying to fix everything, lost the first two chapters of my short story, lost all the drafts that I had written, and had to send my laptop to the mechanic’s.

So long story short: DO NOT EVER TURN OFF YOUR SECURE BOOT
