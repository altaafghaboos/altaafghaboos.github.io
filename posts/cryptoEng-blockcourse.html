Cryptographic Engineering Block Course

i've been there!

During the week of the 12th of January 2026, I attended the week-long block course on Cryptographic Engineering held by Dr. Peter Schwabe (and his Ph.D students Hien Pham and Amin Abdulrahman) at the University of Mauritius.

Let's step back a little, who's Peter Schwabe?

Peter Schwabe, or like I like to call him, the cryptographic rockstar, is one of the minds behing ML-KEM, the NIST standard for quantum-resistant key exchange. It is vastly more resistant to attacks from quantum computers [than RSA], and uses a lattice-algebraic algorithm. It may not be in use now, but once quantum computers become reliable, you'll all be glad that we have ML-KEM.

So what did we do? We learnt about cryptography, the mathemathics behind cryptography, and how to engineer our own cryptographic software — and especially in the advantages in using jasmin instead of c.

I'll need to do a tech take away on that one day; it's so interesting, even though most of the things flew over my head during the course, but for the time being, I'll link Peter's blog here.
