One score minus eleven months ago, our father in networking brought forth on this boy, a new assignment, conceived in... okay enough about me LARPing as Abraham Lincoln now; 9 months ago (I'm writing this in July 2026), the assignment for the networking module was to write up a report on setting up an enterprise network, suffice to say, ya boy was ecstatic. Networking's my jam. I *live* for the networks. I'm a hundred percent in the cult of Tanenbaum, and I for one, am ready to welcome the new emperor of Mankind.

Okay, yeah, so, why 60 pages? You know why? Because once I started writing, I couldn't stop. I had to delve into every little thing, every little nook and crany about it, I could not not include it in there. We were told that there were no page limit, and I took that as an invitation lol. Okay not exactly, we were told that there were no page limit, "mais aster pa submit ene report 100 pages kan mem" ("don't submit, like, 100 pages" for you folks who don't speak creole), and I guess mission accomplished? 60 is nowhere near 100... although he did say that the average report he corrected was 20 pages... (he here is Dr. Vara Appavoo btw).

So, yeah, I was the group leader, me and my friends [Egamben] Pavalaydon and Faizaan [Immambocus] took three weeks to complete it, one of which I almost didn't sleep at all. The crunch is real ugh.

My contribtution to that Bible was coordinating the team, setting up meetings and deadlines, and delegating tasks. As for what I tangibly contributed to the report, theres:
> writing the requirements (network req and subnet policies),
> network design and specification
> security specifications
> usage policies
> network implementation
> network testing
> network evaluation
> notes and troubleshooting
> recommendations and improvements
